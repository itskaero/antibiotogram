⚕ 2024–2025 CLINICAL HIGHLIGHTS & RESISTANCE UPDATES
🔴 NDM-PA (ST235) — Critical 2025
NDM-producing Pseudomonas aeruginosa (ST235 clone) now detected across SE Asia, Middle East, and Europe. Resistant to ALL β-lactams, carbapenems, fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides. Aztreonam-Avibactam (Emblaveo™) is the key agent — aztreonam resists MBL hydrolysis; avibactam blocks co-harbored ESBL/KPC. Cefiderocol (siderophore cephalosporin) active via iron-transporter uptake. Combination therapy recommended; ID specialist mandatory.
🔴 LRSA — Linezolid-Resistant Staph. aureus
cfr, optrA, and poxtA genes confer resistance to linezolid and tedizolid. LRSA isolates increasingly found in livestock-associated settings, long-term care, and hospitals (Spain, Denmark, Brazil, China). Options are extremely limited: Oritavancin, Daptomycin + Ceftaroline (synergistic for VISA/VRSA too), Eravacycline. Vancomycin may be co-resistant (VRSA/VISA overlap). Culture + susceptibility testing is mandatory.
🔴 VRSA / VISA — Vancomycin-Resistant Staph. aureus
VRSA (vanA from enterococci; MIC ≥16) remains rare but devastating. VISA (MIC 4–8) more common, often missed. Both resistant to vancomycin, often linezolid (overlap clones). Daptomycin + Ceftaroline is synergistic gold standard. Oritavancin shows activity against VISA. TP5 glycan-thickening mechanism. ICU sepsis mortality >50%.
⚠ CRE — Community Spread of OXA-48
OXA-48 and OXA-244 producing K. pneumoniae / E. coli increasingly detected in community-onset UTIs (2024 EU surveillance). Travel-associated from Turkey, N. Africa, Indian subcontinent. Key: C/A active only if no co-ESBL; M/V limited. Cefiderocol retains activity. Combination (C/A + Fosfomycin; or Cefiderocol + TGC) for serious infections. Check for co-resistance to polymyxins.
⚠ KPC C/A Resistance Emerging
Ceftazidime-Avibactam resistance in KPC-producing K. pneumoniae now well-documented via: (1) KPC mutation (Asp179Tyr etc.), (2) OmpK35/36 porin loss, (3) MBL co-acquisition. Backup options: Cefiderocol, Meropenem-Vaborbactam (if no MBL), Aztreonam-Avibactam (if NDM). Repeat susceptibility testing of all CRE isolates during therapy.
✦ New Agents 2020–2025
Aztreonam-Avibactam (2024 EU; 2023 FDA for NDM): the MBL solution. Cefiderocol: siderophore cephalosporin, CRAB/XDR-PA/NDM active. Sulbactam-Durlobactam (2023): CRAB-specific. Omadacycline: oral/IV, MRSA + atypicals. Delafloxacin: MRSA-active FQ. Oritavancin/Dalbavancin: long-acting glycopeptides, single-dose SSTI. Plazomicin: CRE-active aminoglycoside.
ℹ CRAB Treatment — Sulbactam is Key
Carbapenem-resistant Acinetobacter baumannii (CRAB): OXA-23/24/58 ubiquitous. Sulbactam (in Ampicillin-Sulbactam or novel Sulbactam-Durlobactam) inhibits PBP2 directly — it is the active agent, not the β-lactam. Cefiderocol + Colistin + Rifampin triple therapy for severe infections. Avoid monotherapy. Tigecycline + Colistin combination used but toxicity significant. Sulbactam-Durlobactam (FDA 2023) is now preferred where available.
ℹ Enterobacter — Never Use Narrow Cephalosporins
Enterobacter cloacae complex and Serratia marcescens harbor inducible AmpC. 1st/2nd/3rd-gen cephalosporins (including ceftriaxone) may appear susceptible in vitro but select de-repressed mutants during therapy → clinical failure. Use Cefepime (high dose 2g q8h), carbapenems, or new BL/BLI. ESBL co-production in ECC rising sharply 2024 (ECDC report).
